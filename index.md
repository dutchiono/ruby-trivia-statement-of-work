# Ruby Trivia — Statement of Work & Value Delivered

*Prepared by **Dutch (`dutchiono`)** — the developer who designed and built Ruby Trivia. Figures are sourced from public GitHub commit history and standard US industry rates; full methodology and the complete commit log are in the [downloadable workbook](Dutch-Ruby-Comp-Claim.xlsx).*

## What I built (shipped and live in production)
- **Trivia PWA** — daily quiz, practice, friend challenges, leaderboards, streaks, ranks/badges, **four languages**, installable to phone.
- **Live multiplayer** — realtime WebSocket lobbies, matchmaking, graceful reconnect.
- **Backend & API** — Hono/Bun server, auth/registration, game logic, and a full **SQLite → MariaDB migration** with a SQL-translation layer.
- **Web3 holder layer** — Solana wallet connect, holder verification, and perks (score multiplier, holders-only leaderboard, cosmetics) on its own subdomain.
- **On-chain / contracts** — contract suite, a result-signer seam, and token-integration support.
- **Telegram bot gateway**, **production infrastructure** (multi-container, nginx, MariaDB, deploy scripts, live incident response), **app-store repackaging**, **token-launch support**, and an **educational variant**.

## The receipts (public commit history)
- **127 commits** across **6 Ruby repositories** under my GitHub identity, **2026-05-29 → 2026-06-26**.
- **13 active build days**, peaking into a launch crunch — **31 commits on launch day (June 24)** alone, through a two-day, no-sleep launch.
- Tracked build time (a documented **floor**, excluding design, testing, launch-night ops, and pre-commit work): **~82–106 hours.**

## What the delivered work is worth
The shipped product represents roughly **770 hours of professional work** (itemized in the workbook). Valued at any rate:

| Valuation basis | Value of delivered work |
|---|---|
| At **$10/hr** (a poverty wage — to make the point) | **$7,700** |
| At **$50/hr** (my agreed rate) | **$38,500** |
| At **~$130/hr** (market senior full-stack / web3, itemized) | **$101,200** |

## What I was offered
> A **flat fee of $3,000–$6,000.**

Even valued at a **$10/hr poverty wage**, the work delivered is worth more than that. At my agreed rate it is roughly **6–13×** more. At market it is a **six-figure product** — meaning the offer was **pennies on the dollar** of what was built.

## The point
I built a **live, six-figure product** and was offered a **few thousand dollars.** This page documents the work and its value plainly, so the gap is a matter of record rather than argument.
